
# Listening and Active Communication

## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1 
### Steps / Strategies to do Active Listening

1. Give full attention to the speaker and avoid distractions.
2. Maintain eye contact and positive body language.
3. Do not interrupt while the other person is speaking.
4. Listen to understand, not just to reply.
5. Ask questions if something is not clear.
6. Summarize important points to confirm understanding.

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2
### Key Points of Reflective Listening (Fisher’s Model)

- Carefully listen to the speaker.
- Understand both the message and the emotion.
- Paraphrase the message in your own words.
- Confirm with the speaker that you understood correctly.

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3
### Obstacles in My Listening Process

- Thinking about my reply while the other person is still talking.
- Getting distracted by phone or surroundings.
- Assuming I already know what the person will say.
- Losing focus during long conversations.

### Question 4: How I Can Improve My Listening

- Keep phone away during conversations.
- Focus completely on the speaker.
- Avoid interrupting.
- Take notes in important discussions.


## 4. Types of Communication

### Question 5
### When Do I Switch to Passive Communication?

- When I want to avoid arguments.
- When talking to seniors or authority figures.
- When I feel my opinion may create conflict.
- In uncomfortable situations.

### Question 6
### When Do I Switch to Aggressive Communication?

- When I feel unheard.
- When I feel disrespected.
- When I am stressed or frustrated.
- When things are not going as expected.

### Question 7
### When Do I Use Passive-Aggressive Communication?

- When I am upset but avoid direct confrontation.
- When I use sarcasm instead of speaking clearly.
- When I ignore someone instead of discussing the issue.
- When I indirectly show anger.

### Question 8
### How Can I Make My Communication Assertive?

- Speak clearly and confidently without shouting.
- Express opinions respectfully.
- Use “I feel” statements instead of blaming.
- Listen carefully before responding.
- Maintain calm tone and body language.
